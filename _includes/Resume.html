Research Experience

ETH Zürich / Biomedical Informatics Lab  / Research Assistant

April  2016 - PRESENT

I am currently at ETH Zürich from my advisor from MSKCC to ETH Zürich. My research is focused on using and extending statistical models to understand transcriptional dysregulation in cancer. In addition to continuing my research projects from MSKCC, described below, I began  two new projects: 1) Method to define  hypoxic signatures in TCGA samples; 2) Method to estimate protein abundance from MS-SWATH data.

Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (MSKCC) / Rätsch Lab / Research assistant

June  2014 - April 2016

My research is focused on understanding transcriptional dysregulation in cancer. During my time at MSKCC, I was able to work on multiple data modalities, such as RNA-Seq and Ribosome Footprinting, to perform statistical analyses to estimate changes in expression, splicing, and translational efficiency. I also participated in the International Cancer Genome Consortium (ICGC) where I integrate multiple transcriptional aberrations such as splicing, fusions, over/under expression, allele specific expression, and others in over 1,000 samples to identify cancer relevant genes and alteration patterns.

Cornell University / Keinan Lab / Rotation Student

As a rotation student I helped to conducted comprehensive genome-wide analysis to research the genetics of Bene Israel peoples from India and determine extent of both Jewish and Indian ancestry.
